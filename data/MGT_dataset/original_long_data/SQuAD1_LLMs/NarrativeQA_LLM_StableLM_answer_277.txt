In the passage, the tribes mentioned were the mi'kmaq and the abenaki, who were engaged in father le loutre's war and held sway in parts of nova scotia, acadia, and the eastern portions of the province of canada, as well as much of present-day maine. The iroquois confederation dominated much of present-day upstate new york and the ohio country, although the latter also included algonquian-speaking populations of delaware and shawnee, as well as iroquoian-speaking mingo.
