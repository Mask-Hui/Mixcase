In what became known as the st. Bartholomew's day massacre of 24 august – 3 october 1572, catholics killed thousands of huguenots in paris. Similar massacres took place in other towns in the weeks following. The main provincial towns and cities experiencing the massacre were aix, bordeaux, bourges, lyons, meaux, orleans, rouen, toulouse, and troyes.
