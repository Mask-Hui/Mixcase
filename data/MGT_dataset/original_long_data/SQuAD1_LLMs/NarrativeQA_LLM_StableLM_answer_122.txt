In botswana, the gender pay gap is a notable and persistent gap in favor of males in the labor market. This gap is attributed to several factors other than discrimination, including differences in education, job opportunities, and access to financial services. According to the world bank, in botswana, the average monthly income for females is approximately $140, while the average monthly income for males is $270. This gap is significant, as it represents a significant disparity in terms of wealth and social status.
