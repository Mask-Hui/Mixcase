In 1523, luther wrote that jesus christ was born a jew which discouraged mistreatment of the jews and advocated their conversion by proving that the old testament could be shown to speak of jesus christ. However, as the reformation continued, luther began to lose hope in large-scale jewish conversion to christianity. In his later years, luther grew more hostile toward the jews, writing against them with the kind of venom he had already unleashed on the anabaptists, zwinglianism, and the papacy.
