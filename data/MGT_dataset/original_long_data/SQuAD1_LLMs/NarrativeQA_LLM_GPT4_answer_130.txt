The passage states that the current 8-4-4 system was launched in 1985 to put more emphasis on vocational subjects, assuming that the new structure would enable school drop-outs at all levels to be self-employed or secure employment in the informal sector. In 2003, free primary education was introduced in kenya. As a result, primary school enrolment increased by about 70%. However, secondary and tertiary education enrolment has not increased proportionally because payment is still required for attendance. In 2007, the government issued a statement declaring that from 2008, secondary education would be heavily subsidised with the government footing all tuition fees.
