In 1784, john wesley broke with traditional practice and ordained two of his lay preachers as presbyters, thomas vasey and richard whatcoat. Dr. Thomas coke, already an anglican priest, was also ordained as a methodist priest and dispatched with vasey and whatcoat to america to take charge of methodist activities there. Wesley and coke met with the representatives of the american colonies to discuss the need to ordain a methodist clergyman.
