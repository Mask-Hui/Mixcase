Charles richard, the inventor of the steam engine indicator, developed the most successful indicator for his high-speed engine, which he exhibited at the london exhibition in 1862. The indicator was a wax-coated paper sheet containing a thin layer of mercury. The indicator was connected to a small lever on the engine, which caused the mercury to flow out of a tube and into a small glass tube attached to a small brass or bronze pointer. The pointer was then connected to a scale on a stand, which allowed the operator to read the pressure in the cylinder.
