In geraets-smits v stichting ziekenfonds, the court of justice held that the freedom to provide services is directly effective and that the dutch health authorities regarded the treatment as unnecessary. The court has recognized that providing health care is a service, and has held that secondary education falls outside the scope of article 56 because usually the state funds it, although higher education does not. The court has also held that health care is a service, and that the netherlands' regulation of cannabis consumption, including the prohibitions by some municipalities on tourists, does not fall outside article 56.
