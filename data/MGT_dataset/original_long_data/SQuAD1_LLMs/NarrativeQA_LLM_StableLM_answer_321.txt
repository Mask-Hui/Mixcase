In 1894, tesla began investigating what he referred to as radiant energy of "invisible" kinds after he had noticed damaged film in his laboratory in previous experiments (later identified as "roentgen rays" or "x-rays"). His early experiments were with crookes tubes, a cold cathode electrical discharge tube. Later in his career, much of tesla's research—hundreds of invention models, plans, notes, laboratory data, tools, photographs, valued at $50,000—was lost in the 5th avenue laboratory fire of march 1895.
