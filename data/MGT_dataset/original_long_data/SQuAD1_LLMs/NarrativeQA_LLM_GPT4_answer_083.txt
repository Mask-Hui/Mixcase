A 1996 study by perotti examined the channels through which inequality may affect economic growth. He showed that, in accordance with the credit market imperfection approach, inequality is associated with lower levels of human capital formation (education, experience, and apprenticeship) and higher levels of fertility, which are reflected in lower rates of growth. He also found that inequality is associated with higher levels of redistributive taxation, which are associated with lower levels of growth from reductions in private savings and investment.
