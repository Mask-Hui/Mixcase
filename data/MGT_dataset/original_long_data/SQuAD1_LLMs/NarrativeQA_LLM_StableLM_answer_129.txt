Under the law of supply and demand, the price of skill is determined by a race between the demand for the skilled worker and the supply of the skilled worker. This means that wages can be considered as a function of market price of skill. Markets can also concentrate wealth, pass environmental costs on to society, and abuse workers and consumers. These outcomes are widely viewed as unfair. Employers who offer a below market wage will find that their business is chronically understaffed.
