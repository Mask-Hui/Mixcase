Following their loss in the divisional round of the previous season's playoffs, the denver broncos underwent numerous coaching changes, including a mutual parting with head coach john fox and the hiring of gary kubiak. Under kubiak, the broncos planned to install a run-oriented offense with zone blocking to blend in with quarterback peyton manning's shotgun passing skills, but struggled with numerous changes and injuries to the offensive line, as well as manning having his worst statistical season since his rookie year with the indianapolis colts in 1998, due to a plantar fasciitis injury in his heel that he had suffered since the summer. Although the team had a 7–0 start, manning led the nfl in interceptions. In week 10, manning suffered a partial tear of the plantar fasciitis in his left foot. He set the nfl's all-time record for career passing yards in this game, but was benched after throwing four interceptions in favor of backup quarterback brock osweiler, who took over as the starter for most of the remainder of the regular season.
