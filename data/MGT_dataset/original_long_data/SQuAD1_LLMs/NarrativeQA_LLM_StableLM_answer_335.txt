The first recorded settlement in what is now newcastle was pons aelius, a roman fort and bridge across the river tyne. It was given the family name of the roman emperor hadrian, who founded it in the 2nd century ad. This rare honour suggests that hadrian may have visited the site and instituted the bridge on his tour of britain. The population of pons aelius at this period was estimated at 2,000.
