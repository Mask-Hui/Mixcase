The primary law of the eu consists mainly of the founding treaties, the "core" treaties being the treaty on european union (teu) and the treaty on the functioning of the european union (tfeu). The treaties contain formal and substantive provisions, which frame policies of the european union institutions and determine the division of competences between the european union and its member states. The teu establishes that european union law applies to the metropolitan territories of the member states, as well as certain islands and overseas territories, including madeira, the canary islands and the french overseas departments.
