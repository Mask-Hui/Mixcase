The african great lakes region, which kenya is a part of, has been inhabited by humans since the lower paleolithic period. By the first millennium ad, the bantu expansion had reached the area from west-central africa. The borders of the modern state consequently comprise the crossroads of the niger-congo, nilo-saharan and afroasiatic areas of the continent, representing most major ethnolinguistic groups found in africa.
