According to the passage, the drought in 2005 in the amazon basin was the worst in a hundred years, and it was likely the second year of a second consecutive year of drought. A study in 2006 suggested that the forest in the amazon basin could survive only three years of drought. This suggests that the forest is on the brink of being turned into savanna or desert, with catastrophic consequences for the world's climate. The study argues that this drought response, coupled with the effects of deforestation on regional climate, are pushing the rainforest towards a "tipping point" where it would irreversibly start to die.
