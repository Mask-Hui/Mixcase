'in contrast to the views of john calvin and philipp melanchthon, throughout his life luther maintained that it was not false doctrine to believe that a christian's soul sleeps after it is separated from the body in death; and, accordingly, he disputed traditional interpretations of some bible passages, such as the parable of the rich man and lazarus. This also led luther to reject the idea of torments for the saints: "it is enough for us to know that souls do not leave their bodies to be threatened by the torments and punishments of hell, but enter a prepared bedchamber in which they sleep in peace
