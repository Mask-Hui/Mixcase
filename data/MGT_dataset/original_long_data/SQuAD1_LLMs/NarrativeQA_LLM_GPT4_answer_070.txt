The passage states that on 17 december 1941, seven protestant regional church confederations issued a statement agreeing with the nazi policy of forcing jews to wear yellow arm bands. The statement was made after luther's bitter experience of the persecution and expulsion of jews from german territory. The statement was made shortly after kristallnacht, a day when synagogues were burned in germany. Bishop martin sasse, a leading protestant churchman, published a compilation of luther's writings shortly after kristallnacht, for which daniel goldhagen argued that luther's writing was a "blueprint." sasse applauded the burning of synagogues
