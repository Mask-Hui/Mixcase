The president of kenya is called the president and is both the head of state and head of government. There is a multi-party system in kenya with the president of the kenya being elected for a five-year term. The president is elected by the people of kenya through a system of ballots, called elections, in which a political party or alliance receives the most votes and most of the seats in the house of representatives. The president is the commander-in-chief of the armed forces and has control of key government institutions including the police, judiciary, administration, communications, customs and the central intelligence organisation. He or she serves as the head of government during the ruling party's term of office, or when that party is not in power.
