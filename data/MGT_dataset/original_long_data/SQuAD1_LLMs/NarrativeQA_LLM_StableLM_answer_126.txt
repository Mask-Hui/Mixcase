The neighborhood of brookhaven includes kearney boulevard, named after early 20th century entrepreneur and millionaire m. Theo kearney, which extends from fresno street in southwest fresno about 20 mi (32 km) west to kerman, california. A small, two-lane rural road for most of its length, kearney boulevard is lined with tall palm trees. The roughly half-mile stretch of kearney boulevard between fresno street and thorne ave was at one time the preferred neighborhood for fresno's elite african-american families.
