The term "imperialism" was first introduced into english in its current sense around the late 1870s by opponents of british prime minister benjamin disraeli's allegedly aggressive and oppressive imperial policies. It was shortly appropriated by supporters of "imperialism," such as joseph chamberlain. The term has since been associated with a variety of meanings, including political self-interest and capitalist greed. However, the concept of imperialism as a structural feature of capitalism has been emphasized by later marxist theorists, such as vladimir lenin.
