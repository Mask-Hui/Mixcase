What did the works council directive require? the works council directive (wcd) was a european union law passed in 1996, which required member states to implement and enforce the national policies agreed under the social chapter of the 1997 treaty of amsterdam. This included the requirement for national policies to include social policies, such as the social chapter of the 1997 treaty of amsterdam. The wcd also established the european employment agency (eea) and set out specific requirements for employers to implement social policies.
