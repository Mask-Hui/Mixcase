In 2009, joseph stiglitz presented evidence in which he argued that both global inequality and inequality within countries prevented growth by limiting aggregate demand. This statement suggests that if countries are unable to grow, they will not be able to provide resources to their citizens, leading to a lack of demand for goods and services. This idea is supported by the argument that if inequality were to persist, there would be limited demand for goods and services, leading to a decline in economic growth.
