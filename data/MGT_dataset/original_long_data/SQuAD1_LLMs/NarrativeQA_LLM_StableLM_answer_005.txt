In cases where the eu's basic constitutional rights and principles are not in conflict with german law, the eu can override german law. This includes cases where the eu's fundamental principles of democracy, the rule of law, and the social state principles are not being effectively protected by the eu. However, as the uk supreme court noted in r (hs2 action alliance ltd) v secretary of state for transport, the uk constitution is uncodified and parliament did not either contemplate or authorise the abrogation of those principles when it enacted the european communities act 1972.
