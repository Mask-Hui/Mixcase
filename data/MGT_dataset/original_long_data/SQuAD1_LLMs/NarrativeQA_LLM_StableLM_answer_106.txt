In 1893, george westinghouse won the bid to light the 1893 world's columbian exposition in chicago with alternating current, beating out a general electric bid by one million dollars. This world's fair devoted a building to electrical exhibits. It was a key event in the history of ac power, as westinghouse demonstrated the safety, reliability, and efficiency of a fully integrated alternating current system to the american public.
