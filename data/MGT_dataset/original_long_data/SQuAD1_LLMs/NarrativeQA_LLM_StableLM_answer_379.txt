The passage states that the grainger market was originally built in 1808 and was newcastle's first indoor market. It was opened in 1835 and was newcastle's first indoor market. The market is largely in its original condition, with the exception of the timber roof which was destroyed by a fire in 1901 and replaced by latticed-steel arches. The grainger market architecture, like most in grainger town, which are either grade i or ii listed, was listed grade i in 1954 by english heritage.
