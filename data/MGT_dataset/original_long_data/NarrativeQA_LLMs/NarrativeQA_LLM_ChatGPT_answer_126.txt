What is "bleak house" about? "bleak house" is a novel by charles dickens that explores the themes of corruption and injustice in the english legal system through the story of the chancery case of jarndyce and jarndyce. The novel follows the lives of several characters, including esther summerson, who discovers that she is the illegitimate daughter of lady dedlock and captain hawdon, and becomes the ward of john jarndyce. Esther, along with her cousins richard carstone and ada clare, is a beneficiary in the disputed will that forms the basis of the jarndyce case. As the legal battle drags on for years, the lives of the characters become intertwined in various ways, with tragic consequences for some. The novel is also a portrait of victorian society, with its stark class divisions and social inequalities.
