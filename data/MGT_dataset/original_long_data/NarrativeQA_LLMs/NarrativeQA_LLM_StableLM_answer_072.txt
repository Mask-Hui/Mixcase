Socrates answers that one should not worry about public opinion, but only listen to wise and expert advice. He also introduces the voice of the laws of athens, which speaks to him and proceeds to explain why it would be unjust for him to leave his cell. Since the laws exist as one entity, to break one would be to break them all, and in doing so, socrates would cause them great harm. The citizen is bound to the laws like a child is bound to a parent, and so to go against the laws would be like striking a parent. Rather than simply break the laws and escape, socrates should try to persuade the laws
