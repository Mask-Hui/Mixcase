Frank saltram offered nothing in return for the trust and friendship he had built with them, except for conversation. He was not a conman or a fraud, but rather a man with a real intellect and a future in politics. He was ungrateful and unreliable, and was prone to immoral acts. Saltram lived off others, particularly the mulvilles, who were convinced of his genius and enjoyed his talk.
