What is the main conflict in the novel "bleak house" by charles dickens? the main conflict in "bleak house" by charles dickens is the lawsuit of jarndyce and jarndyce, which has been dragging on for generations, causing destruction and ruin to the lives of those involved. The lawsuit involves a disputed inheritance and several beneficiaries, including the three wards of john jarndyce - esther summerson, ada clare, and richard carstone. The conflict is further complicated by the involvement of lawyers and other interested parties, each with their own motives and interests. The lawsuit and its consequences are the central theme of the novel, and it serves as a commentary on the flaws of the legal system and the harm that can result from prolonged litigation.
