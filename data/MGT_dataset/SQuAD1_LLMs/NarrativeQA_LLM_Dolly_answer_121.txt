Stephen Eilmann is suggesting that in order to be a good citizen of the society one should not commit a crime. He is assuming that to be a good citizen of the society one should not do anything that is forbidden by the society. However, in the passage 'A Primer for Prospective Jurors' of Fully Informed Jury Association, it is mentioned that there is a moral prohibition on deception.
