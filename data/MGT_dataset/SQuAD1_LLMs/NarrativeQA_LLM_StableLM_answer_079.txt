In the autumn of 1991, the talks for the broadcast rights for the Premier League for a five-year period, from the 1992 season, were held.  ITV were the current rights holders, and fought hard to retain the new rights.  ITV had increased their offer from £18m to £34m per year to keep control of the rights.  BSkyB joined forces with the BBC to make a counter bid.
