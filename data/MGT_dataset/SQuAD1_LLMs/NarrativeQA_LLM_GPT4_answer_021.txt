The Yuan dynasty promoted cultural exchange between East and West through its extensive contacts with the Mongol Empire, which had significant influence on China. The Mongol Empire's West Asian and European contacts produced a fair amount of cultural exchange, which had significantly eased trade and commerce across Asia until its decline. The communications between the Yuan dynasty and its ally in Persia, the Ilkhanate, encouraged this development. Buddhism had a great influence in the Yuan government, and Tibetan-rite Tantric Buddhism had significantly influenced China during this period.
