Ctenophores are a group of colonial marine animals that live in the oceans. Their body plan is unusual, with a flattened and segmented body with a central tube, a ring of tentacles, and a mouth at the end. The mouth is usually wide, allowing them to eat and drink, but in some species the tentacles can be retracted into the body. The mouth is surrounded by a ring of cilia, which are small hairs that move in response to the cilia on the tentacles.
