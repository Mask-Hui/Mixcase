The well-known archeologist who believed the Amazon didn't have many inhabitants is Betty Meggers. She was a prominent proponent of the 'Amazonia: Man and Culture in a Counterfeit Paradise'. Archeologist Betty Meggers believed that a population density of 0.2 inhabitants per square kilometre (0.52/sq mi) is the maximum that can be sustained in the rainforest through hunting, with agriculture needed to host a larger population.
