The Harvard Corporation (formally, the President and Fellows of Harvard College) is its first chartered corporation. Harvard was established in 1636 by King Charles II and was the first university in the English-speaking world to be established for the purpose of educating and training men for the ministry. Although it was originally founded for the purpose of funding Harvard College, it became more general over the course of its history. In the early 18th century, Harvard began to gradually secularize its curriculum and by the 19th century, Harvard had emerged as the most prominent institution of higher learning in the United States.
