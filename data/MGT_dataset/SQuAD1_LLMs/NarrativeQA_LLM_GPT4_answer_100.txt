The revocation of the Edict forbidding Protestant services, required education of children as Catholics and prohibited emigration, proved disastrous for the Huguenots and costly for France. It precipitated civil bloodshed, ruined commerce and resulted in the illegal flight from the country of hundreds of thousands of Protestants, many becoming intellectuals and business leaders in Britain as well as Holland, Prusia and South Africa. Four thousand emigrated to the North American colonies, where they settled in New York and Virginia.
