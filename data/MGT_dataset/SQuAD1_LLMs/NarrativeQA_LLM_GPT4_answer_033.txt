The plague had a significant impact on the Middle East. The disease spread to various countries in the region, leading to serious depopulation and permanent change in both economic and social structures. The disease entered the region from southern Russia and reached Alexandria in Egypt, probably through the port's trade with Constantinople. The disease travelled eastward to Gaza, north along the eastern coast of Syria and Palestine to cities such as Ashkalon, Acre, Jerusalem, Sidon, Damascus, Homs and Aleppo.
