Sophocles' play Antigone is the Greek tragedy about the life of Oedipus, who was killed by his own brother, and is considered one of the classic works of Greek literature. The play is based on the myth of Oedipus, and it explores the themes of morality, fate, and the nature of human responsibility. Antigone is the daughter of Thebes, a city-state that was conquered by the Greeks, and she defies the authority of the King of Thebes, who is trying to stop her from fulfilling her duty as a citizen by giving Polynices a proper burial.
