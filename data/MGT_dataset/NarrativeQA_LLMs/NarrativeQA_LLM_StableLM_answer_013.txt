Hypatia the pagan philosopher and Cyril the Christian patriarch meet in a lecture at the University of Alexandria, where she lectures on the history of the Hebrews and the teachings of the Christian church. Philammon, a devoted follower of Hypatia's teachings, is her most enthusiastic disciple and becomes her closest friend. However, despite her affection for him, she also expresses a deep-seated hatred for Christianity.
