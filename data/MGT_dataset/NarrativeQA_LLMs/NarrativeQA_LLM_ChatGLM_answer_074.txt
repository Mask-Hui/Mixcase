Vervelle is impressed with Grassou because he is introduced to him as a grand master, and he is impressed by Grassou's ability to resell forgeries for a large profit. He also believes that Grassou possess all the combined talent of famous artists such as Rubens, Rembrandt, Terburg, and Titian, despite his own mediocrity. Despite his advantageous marriage, Grassou remains bitter that he is not a true artist.
