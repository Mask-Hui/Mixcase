The art-dealer describes Grassou's skill level as a grand master, even though Grassou himself recognizes his mediocre painting skills. Magus has introduced Grassou to Vervelle as a grand master to impress them and gain their favor. Despite his mediocre painting skills, Vervelle believes that Grassou possess the combined talent of Rubens, Rembrandt, Terburg, and Titian, which multiples his value as an artist and as a son-in-law.
