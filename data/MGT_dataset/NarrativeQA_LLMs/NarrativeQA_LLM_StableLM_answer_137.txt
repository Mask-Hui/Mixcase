The story The House of the Wolfings is a romantic and idealistic portrayal of the Germanic Gothic tribes, written in an archaic style and incorporating a large amount of poetry. The story portrays the lives of the Germanic tribes as simple and hardworking, living in a forest and worshipping their gods Odin and Tyr by sacrificing horses. The story also shows the division of the Mark, the Upper-mark, the Mid-mark, and the Nether-mark.
