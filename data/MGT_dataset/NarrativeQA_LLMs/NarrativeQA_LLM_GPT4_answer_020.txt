The protagonist of the story is Paul Darnley, an intellectual who becomes disillusioned with his own beliefs and values. He is a character who undergoes significant transformation throughout the novel, from a materialist and atheist to an advocate for the "Religion of Humanity." The story also explores the theme of utopia and its limitations, as well as the dangers of intellectual arrogance and materialism. Ultimately, the story concludes with Paul's realization that his wife has achieved her goal of finding him, and that he himself has been transformed by his experiences.
