Socrates tells Crito not to worry about public opinion, but only listen to wise and expert advice. He also introduces the voice of the Laws of Athens, which speaks to him and explains why it would be unjust for Socrates to leave his cell. The Laws exist as one entity, and to break them would be like striking a parent. Rather than simply break the Laws and escape, Socrates should try to persuade them to let him go. These Laws present the citizen's duty to them in a kind of social contract. By choosing to live in Athens, a citizen is implicitly endorsing the Laws and willing to abide by them. Socrates
